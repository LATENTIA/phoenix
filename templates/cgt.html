Belgian CGT 2026+, rules in plain language

1. The new regime in one paragraph

Starting {{ regime_start }}, Belgium taxes net realised capital gains on financial assets at a flat {{ "%.0f"|format(tax_rate_pct) }}%. There is a generous annual exemption (€{{ "{:,.0f}".format(annual_exemption) }}) plus a rollover bank, and pre-existing positions get a basis reset to their {{ reset_date }} market value so only the gain accumulated after that date is in scope. Realisations on or before {{ reset_date }} stay exempt under the transitional rule.

2. Headline mechanics

  • Rate: {{ "%.0f"|format(tax_rate_pct) }}% flat on net realised gains per calendar year (gains minus losses, same year only).
  • Annual exemption: €{{ "{:,.0f}".format(annual_exemption) }} per taxpayer, applied after netting.
  • Carry-forward bank: up to €{{ "{:,.0f}".format(max_yearly_carry) }}/year of unused base exemption rolls forward, capped at €{{ "{:,.0f}".format(max_carry_bank) }} total. Phoenix tracks this balance year by year (see the Annual summary tab, "Carry in" and "Carry out" columns).
  • Loss offset: losses cancel gains 1:1 within the same calendar year only. No carry-forward of losses to future years.
  • Transitional exemption: every realisation dated on or before {{ reset_date }} is exempt. The dashboard's Annual summary tab shows them as zero-tax rows for transparency.

3. The {{ reset_date }} basis reset (favorable to taxpayer)

For lots opened before {{ regime_start }} and sold afterward, Phoenix uses

tax basis = max(original cost, {{ reset_date }} market value)

This implements the favorable-to-the-taxpayer rule: the original buy basis is preserved if it would produce a smaller taxable gain than the reset would. The favorable rule is available for 5 years post-enactment.

4. End-to-end math (one taxable year)

Two stocks closed in 2027. One pre-2026 lot, one post-reset lot:

Lot A: bought 2024-03 at €5,000, marked at {{ reset_date }} = €7,500, sold 2027 at €9,000 basis = max(5000, 7500) = €7,500
Lot A realised gain €9,000 − €7,500 = +€1,500
Lot B: bought 2027 at €4,000, sold 2027 at €3,500 (post-reset, original basis) basis = €4,000
Lot B realised loss €3,500 − €4,000 = −€500
Net for the year (gain − loss) €1,500 − €500 = €1,000
Annual exemption applied (€{{ "{:,.0f}".format(annual_exemption) }}) −€1,000 (capped at net)
Taxable amount €0 → €0 tax

The €{{ "{:,.0f}".format(annual_exemption) }} − €1,000 = €9,000 of unused base exemption is partly banked: up to €{{ "{:,.0f}".format(max_yearly_carry) }} rolls over into next year's pool. The bank caps at €{{ "{:,.0f}".format(max_carry_bank) }} total.

5. Forced closes (delistings, mergers, Chapter 11)

Bankruptcies, delistings, and cash mergers are treated identically to ordinary trade losses: the cost basis (or its reset) becomes the loss, which offsets gains in the same year. Phoenix tags these rows forced close in the per-trade view so the accountant can see the breakdown without the math changing.

For multi-step bankruptcy reorganisations (a stock entering Chapter 11 and getting a "Q" suffix, then later being renamed entirely), Phoenix rolls the basis through the rename as a non-taxable event and only realises the loss on the eventual close. See the Detected renames tab for an audit log of every rollover, with the date, the old/new ticker, and the matched quantity.

6. Year-end mark sourcing

The {{ reset_date }} mark for each held symbol comes from Yahoo Finance via the Refresh year-end marks button on the dashboard. For symbols Yahoo doesn't carry (delisted, OTC pinks, certain ADRs), the calculator falls back to the original buy basis and flags the row original (mark missing). Add the missing prices manually from a broker statement or a paid market-data source if you need exact numbers.

8. Things this report does not do

  • 33% on internal capital gains (sale of shares to a controlled company). Separate special regime, requires per-trade flags.
  • Progressive 0% / 1.25% / 2.5% / 5% / 10% scheme for substantial shareholdings (typically ≥20% ownership). Different rules and paperwork; consult your accountant.
  • 16.5% rate on transfers to non-EEA entities.
  • Other regimes (collective investment schemes with their own withholding mechanics, life-insurance products, derivatives with mark-to-market accounting).

These additional regimes don't trigger from retail IBKR cash-equity activity. If any apply to you, this calculator is incomplete.
